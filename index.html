The Check Engine Light

A loose gas cap can trigger the Check Engine Light. Always tighten it until it clicks. If the light is flashing, it indicates a serious misfire that requires immediate attention.

üîß

Right to Repair

We advocate for your right to choose who repairs your vehicle. Modern cars require complex tools, but legislation is helping ensure independent shops have access.

üëÉ

Diagnostic Smells

Unusual smells can tell a story. The sweet smell of maple syrup often indicates a coolant leak, while burning carpet can point to an issue with your brakes.

üíß

Hygroscopic Fluids

Brake fluid naturally absorbs water from the air over time, which can lead to corrosion and reduced performance. It should be flushed and replaced every 2-3 years.

A Spark of Genius: The Internal Combustion Story

The internal combustion engine (ICE) is a heat engine that converts chemical energy into mechanical energy. The story of the ICE is a fascinating journey of innovation, with key contributions from inventors like Nicolaus Otto, who in 1876, created the first successful four-stroke engine, and Karl Benz, who developed the first automobile powered by an ICE in 1886. This invention revolutionized transportation and paved the way for the modern world.
